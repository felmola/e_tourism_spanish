In this part, there will be a market for tourist packages. The market has a total of 5 negotiation rounds. Before starting the first negotiation round, the computer will randomly assign you to one of two roles: either buyer or seller. Keep in mind that the role to which you were assigned will be maintained during the 5 negotiation rounds.

The objective of the market is that buyers and sellers can negotiate and eventually complete transactions successfully in each of the negotiation rounds. The results of the transactions will affect your final payment.

In this market each commodity is a tourist package. In total there are 5 types of tourist packages whose only difference will be the destination: Berlin, Madrid, Vienna, Paris and Rome.

In each of the negotiation rounds, first the sellers will set prices to the packages they will sell and then the buyers will see a list containing the packages available in the market and the prices of each of these packages. To complete a transaction, buyers must select the package of their preference and purchase it. Bare in mind, that if two buyers pick the same package, the fastest one will be the one who gets to complete the transaction.

Before starting, both sellers and buyers will have an initial endowment of {{Constants.endowment}} (Experimental Currency Units) in each of the trading rounds to make their decisions. The payoff you will see after each of the trading rounds will consider both the initial endowment and the earnings from your decisions

Please click on the "Next" button to start the first round of negotiation.
